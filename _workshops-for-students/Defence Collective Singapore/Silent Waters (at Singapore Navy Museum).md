<h3>Defence Collective Singapore</h3>
<h4>Silent Waters (at Singapore Navy Museum)</h4>
<p>Impressive, stealthy, and scalable. Deep dive into the captivating world
of submarines and how they work underwater to protect economic zones, exercise
deterrence, collect intelligence and make a difference in inter-ally operations.</p>
<p>Explore the science behind the submarines through hands-on exploration
and visit the Singapore Navy Museum’s Permanent Exhibits Gallery to learn
more about what makes the Republic of Singapore Navy a modern and integrated
maritime fighting force - the naval platforms it possesses and the arsenal
that these platforms pack. Singapore Navy Museum is managed by Defence
Collective Singapore.</p>
<p><strong>Details</strong>
</p>
<p><u>INTRODUCTION [30 MINS] </u>
</p>
<p>Students will be introduced to submarines, how they work to defend Singapore’s
interests and the different types of submarines and their uses.</p>
<p><u>PERMANENT EXHIBITS GALLERY VISIT [30 MINS] </u>
</p>
<p>Students will have the opportunity to explore the Navy Museum’s exhibits
gallery to learn more about the Navy’s role in defending Singapore’s waters
and protecting our interests.</p>
<p><u>HANDS-ON EXPLORATION [1 HOUR] </u>
</p>
<p>Students will be tasked to build their own submarines and discover the
relationship between mass, volume, pressure, and density.</p>
<p><strong>Objectives:</strong>
</p>
<p>1. Understand the role that the Navy plays in the defence of Singapore
and how submarines can help to secure Singapore’s trade routes and defence.</p>
<p>2. Learn about the different types of submarines and their uses.</p>
<p>3. Gain insights into how pressure and science play a part in the making
and operationalisation of a submarine.</p>
